My essays

My last posts depend on each other, and should be read in sequence. Articles not in bold are optional.

  • Assignment Statement Considered Harmful. Pervasive mutable state make programs more difficult to deal with. While this is old news, most programmers don't know or don't care. It's a pity, because simple remedies exist.
  • Defining Syntactic Sugar. Most of the time, "Object Oriented" actually means classes. Classes are nothing more than syntactic sug, at least in statically typed languages such as Java and C++. Sugar is not useless, but seeing past it helps us understand classes better.
  • Taboo "OO". The term "OO" is so overloaded that we should stop using it. Better subsitutes are "classes" and "prototypes".
  • How Class-based Programming Sucks. Classes are vastly sub-optimal. Functional programming is far better.
